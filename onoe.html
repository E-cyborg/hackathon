One Nation One Election

Synchronized Lok Sabha & State Assembly elections every 5 years

Historical Context

India practiced simultaneous elections from 1951-1967. Mid-term polls disrupted the cycle.
Proposed restart post-2029 via constitutional amendments.

Countries Practicing Simultaneous Elections

Sweden

All levels synced every 4 years since 1970. National, regional, municipal elections on single day. Voter turnout: 84.2%.

South Africa

National & Provincial legislatures every 5 years. Managed 26.8 million voters successfully in 2024.

Belgium

Federal & Regional elections synchronized. Handles complex federal structure similar to India's diversity.

Nepal

Post-2015 Constitution implemented in phases. Shows feasibility for large, diverse democracies.

Required Constitutional Amendments

Article 83

Lok Sabha Term

Fixed 5-year terms with synchronization clause. Mid-term elections trigger cycle reset for all states.

Article 172

State Assemblies

Aligns state terms with Lok Sabha. Prevents premature dissolution disrupting national cycle.

Article 327/329

Election Commission Powers

ECI authority to notify synchronized poll dates and manage cycle resets.

Implementation Process

1

Two Constitutional Bills

Special majority (2/3rd) in both Houses of Parliament required.

2

State Ratification

50% state legislatures must approve federal structure changes.

3

Cycle Reset 2029

Post-2029 elections restart synchronized cycle for 30+ years.
